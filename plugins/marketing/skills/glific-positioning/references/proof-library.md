## 2. Success narratives (full)

Use one story per asset. Structure when retelling: **the NGO and its mission → the bottleneck → what they did on Glific → the transformation (with the number) → the human detail / quote.** The NGO is the hero throughout.

### MukkaMaar — reaching adolescent girls at scale *(flagship; the "one mega case")*
- **Mission:** self-defence and agency for adolescent girls. *"India has 3 million adolescent girls. I want to reach all of them."* `[deck]`
- **Bottleneck:** scaling offline impact with limited time, cost, and talent.
- **On Glific:** built by a self-described non-technical lead; one chatbot ("Mukki") with multilingual, personalised, AI-powered, integrated features; voice + multiple languages (Hindi, Marathi). `[deck]`
- **Transformation:** **3,000+ girls reached, across 54 schools.** `[deck]` **`[verify]`** — an older version of this story cited different figures (e.g. "113 schools / 2.4M messages"); **use the deck's 3,000+ girls / 54 schools** and do not resurrect the old numbers unless re-confirmed.

- **Quotes:** see §4 (Shraddha).

### Civis — public consultation at population scale

### Sauramandala Foundation — last-mile, locally-illustrated learning

## 5. Pricing facts

Frame in program terms (positioning Finding 2); never as a bare price list. Sources tagged inline.

**How pricing is structured (the story to tell):**
- **One simple platform fee — no feature tiers, no per-seat pricing.** Every plan includes every feature, and users are unlimited: *"You get it all — regardless of the plan you choose."* `[web]`
- **Three billing options:** Annual (lowest cost), Quarterly, Monthly (best for pilots). `[web]`
- A **free trial** is available (contact required). `[web]`

**Platform plan prices** `[deck]` `[verify]` — the live page lists the three billing options but is contact-gated on exact figures; confirm before publishing a specific number:
- **Annual** — ₹8,708/mo (₹1,04,500 billed once a year). *Recommended.* Includes 1 month free.
- **Quarterly** — ₹9,167/mo (₹27,500 every 3 months).
- **Monthly** — ₹9,500/mo.

**One-time setup:** **₹15,000 + taxes (USD 200)** — account setup, team onboarding, platform training. Paid once, not recurring. `[web]` `[deck]`

**What every plan includes** `[web]`: unlimited users (no per-seat); flow builder; multilingual; WhatsApp group automation; dashboards & analytics; AI integration; third-party integrations (Google Sheets, BigQuery, OpenAI, webhooks); secure infrastructure; unlimited Discord/email support.

**Usage-based costs to plan for:**
- **WhatsApp messaging** (via Gupshup BSP), per conversation `[web]`: Marketing **$0.0118**; Utility **$0.0014**; Authentication **$0.0014**; International authentication **$0.0304**; **user-initiated: free**. Typical NGO spend **₹5,000–7,000/month** during pilots (~500 users). `[web]` `[deck]`
- **Google Cloud (BigQuery):** free tier = **10 GB storage + 1 TB queries/month**; ~**90% of NGOs** stay within it initially. `[web]`
- **WhatsApp groups automation:** Maytapi **$30/month**. `[web]`
- **Consulting (optional, tiered):** **₹3,500/hr ($45)** for 0–10 hrs; **₹3,000/hr ($40)** for 10–50 hrs; **₹2,500/hr ($35)** for 50+ hrs. `[web]`

**The pricing-narrative angle (from "no feature tiers, unlimited users"):** the simplest honest line is that an NGO pays one platform fee, gets every feature and unlimited team members, and adds only usage-based messaging on top — which is why the real comparison is against manual operations and total cost of ownership, not a per-seat SaaS sticker.

## 6. Sourcing rules

1. If it isn't in this file or on glific.org/tag/case-study/, it isn't a usable number.
2. One proof point per asset — a list of metrics dilutes all of them.
3. The proof must match the idea the asset leads with (scale benefit → Civis/MukkaMaar/TAP numbers; community differentiator → peer proof; data point → reporting proof).
4. Treat every `[verify]` figure as provisional; confirm before any high-stakes or external-published use.
5. Quotes are verbatim and attributed. Reported speech (ABBF) is presented as reported, not as a quotation.
6. When in doubt, prefer the smaller, defensible number over the bigger, shaky one.
