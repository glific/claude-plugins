# Channel Playbook — Website

Two layers plus a third mode. **Channel optimization** (how a B2B site converts), **messaging adaptation** (how Glific's positioning maps to each page), and an **audit mode** (scoring an existing page against the positioning — the most common request). Pull facts from the backbone docs; this governs form and structure.

## When to use
Writing new website copy, restructuring a page, or **auditing an existing Glific page** against the positioning. For audits, jump to §4.

---

## 1. Channel optimization (how a B2B site converts)

Grounded in conversion-centred design principles:

- **Guide every visitor toward one primary action.** A page with five competing CTAs converts worse than a page with one. Glific's primary action is *Book a call* / *See your program on Glific*; everything else is secondary.
- **Above the fold must answer "what is this, for whom, why care" in ~5 seconds.** The product + the benefit + one proof + one CTA. No scrolling required to understand the offer.
- **Navigation follows the decision stage,** not the org chart. A time-poor program lead should find "how it works," "pricing," "stories," and "book a call" without hunting.
- **Mobile-first — optimize for phone and desktop, weighted to phone.** Most traffic is mobile. Write short, front-loaded lines that don't wrap into a wall on a narrow screen; keep the hero headline to a few words; put the value in the first line; use tap-sized CTAs. Read every line at phone width before you judge it on desktop.
- **Social proof is placed, not piled.** Logos and a peer number near the hero and the CTA, where conviction is forming and where the decision is made.
- **Scannability.** Clear visual hierarchy, short sections, one idea per section, generous whitespace. Visitors scan in an F-pattern before they read.
- **The CTA is repeated** at the natural decision points (after the hero, after proof, at the end) — same action, consistent label.
- **Footer does real work:** sitemap, contact, social, email sign-up, privacy/DPDP, a final CTA.

## 2. Messaging adaptation — page by page

The whole site tells **one story** (messaging §8). Each page is one beat. The descriptor, the lead benefit (time/effort saved, reach at scale), the hero (the NGO), and the problem-as-consequence are the same everywhere. We sell SaaS — the platform and its benefits lead; community appears as a differentiator, in proportion.

| Page / section | Job | Leads with | Pull from |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hero** | Answer "why care" in 5 seconds | The NGO's problem + the benefit + the descriptor + one CTA | messaging §1, §2, §5 |
| **The problem** | Make the program feel seen | The capacity escalation (consequence, not feature) | messaging §2 |
| **What it does** | The benefit, then the "how" | Time/effort saved + reach at scale; the plain 3-step | messaging §1, §3 |
| **Why Glific** | The differentiators | Built-for-nonprofits and the rest, one per block | messaging §4 |
| **The community** | A differentiator, in proportion | The peer network, cohorts, sprints, co-creation | messaging §4, proof-library §2 |
| **How it works** | The plan | The 4-step program journey | messaging §3 |
| **Stories / proof** | Evidence | One mega case (MukkaMaar) + a scale case (Civis/TAP) + sector range | proof-library §2–3 |
| **Credentials** | Trust | Open source, Digital Public Good, DPDP/GDPR | proof-library §1, messaging §5 |
| **Pricing** | Own the cost narrative | Program-terms framing, transparency about layers | messaging §7, proof-library §5 |
| **Final CTA** | Convert | The tagline as a question + Book a call | messaging §5 |

### The hero formula
```
[Headline — the NGO's reality or the benefit, never "Glific is…"]
[Subhead: the value in one line — who it's for + what changes (time saved / reach at scale)]
[ Primary CTA button ]   [secondary, quieter link]
[One proof strip: 120+ NGOs · 10+ sectors · one logo row]
```
Headline options to adapt (never lead the hero with the word "Glific"):
- Problem-led: *"One team. One WhatsApp. Thousands of beneficiaries."*
- Benefit-led: *"Your team's time belongs to your program — not to manual outreach."*
- Scale-led: *"Reach every beneficiary, in their language, at the time they need."*
- Descriptor-led: *"India's most preferred WhatsApp chatbot platform for nonprofits."*
- Tagline-led: *"Reach your beneficiaries where they are, when they need."*

**Never use:** "without growing the team," "without an extra hire," or any scale-benefit framing that implies headcount reduction. The scale benefit is about reach — not staffing. (See positioning.md §8.)

## 3. Writing-new-copy checklist
- Hero answers what/who/why-care above the fold, leads with the benefit, with one CTA.
- The problem is stated as a consequence before any feature appears.
- "What it does" leads with the benefit (time/effort saved, reach at scale); capabilities appear as proof of a value, never as a naked feature grid.
- "Why Glific" carries the differentiators one per block; community is present in proportion, not the headline.
- One mega case (MukkaMaar) plus a scale case and a sector range; every number sourced.
- Pricing framed in program terms with the layers shown honestly.
- One primary CTA, repeated at decision points; secondary links quieter.
- Sage register; no banned phrases; no "X, not Y"; no narrative theater; no "Glific" as the hero's first word.

## 4. Audit mode

When asked to audit an existing page (or the whole site), walk the traceability chain *upward* and score each dimension. Produce a short report: **score, what's working, what's drifting, the specific rewrite.**

### Audit scorecard (score each 0–2: 0 = fails, 1 = partial, 2 = clean)

| # | Dimension | What "clean" looks like |
|---|-----------|------------------------|
| 1 | **Why before what** | The page leads with the NGO's problem and the benefit, not "Glific is a chatbot builder." |
| 2 | **NGO is the hero** | The NGO is the protagonist; Glific is the guide. Glific isn't the subject of most sentences. |
| 3 | **Leads with the benefit** | The primary value (time/effort saved, reach at scale) is the headline. Community appears as a differentiator, in proportion — not as the lead. |
| 4 | **One idea per section** | No section stacks multiple benefits, differentiators, or claims. |
| 5 | **Proof is real & sourced** | Every number traces to proof-library or glific.org; the 120-vs-150 figure is used consistently. |
| 6 | **Consequence framing** | Problems are stated as what the program faces, not as feature gaps. |
| 7 | **Voice (Sage register)** | Program language, measured and explanatory; no banned phrases, no "X, not Y," no narrative theater, no hype openers. |
| 8 | **Single clear CTA** | One primary action, repeated; not five competing CTAs. |
| 9 | **Above-the-fold clarity** | What/who/why-care answered in ~5 seconds, mobile included. |
| 10 | **Descriptor consistency** | The product is described the same way as everywhere else ("…Platform for Nonprofits"). |

**Scoring:** /20. 17–20 on-positioning; 11–16 drifting, fix the flagged dimensions; ≤10 rewrite against the backbone.

### The audit deliverable (this is all the reader gets)

Score each dimension above for your own prioritization — but **deliver only a clean three-column table:** current copy, suggested copy, one short rationale. No score breakdowns, no "what's working" section, no stacked commentary or qualifications. This is an internal doc: clarity over formatting (SKILL.md output discipline).

Lead with one line — the page and a one-phrase verdict (on-positioning / drifting / rewrite) — then the table. That's it.

```
# [page] — [drifting / rewrite]
```

| Current copy | Suggested copy | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "[exact quote from the page]" | "[the on-positioning rewrite]" | [one line: the fix] |

## Website validation (in addition to the three-layer pass)
- Could the hero belong to Wati or Turn.io? If yes, it leads with *what* — rewrite to lead with the NGO and the benefit.
- Does the page lead with the benefit, with community kept in proportion as a differentiator?
- Is there exactly one primary CTA per screen of decision?
- Is the descriptor identical to every other Glific surface ("…Platform for Nonprofits")?
