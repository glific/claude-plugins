# Channel Playbook — Newsletter

Two layers: **channel optimization** (how email earns the open and the read) and **messaging adaptation** (how Glific's positioning flexes for the inbox). Pull facts from the backbone docs; this governs form.

## When to use
Any email newsletter, nurture sequence, or broadcast to NGOs, prospects, or the community.

## Channel optimization (how email works)

- **The subject line is 80% of the job.** ~35–50 characters, one idea, specific, no clickbait. A number or a name beats an adjective. Sentence case reads more human than Title Case.
- **The preheader is the second subject line.** The ~40–90 characters after the subject in the inbox preview — extend the subject, don't repeat it. Never leave it to auto-fill with "View in browser."
- **One email, one job.** A newsletter can have sections, but it has one primary CTA. Every section should ladder toward it.
- **Front-load the point.** Many readers see only the preview pane. The first sentence must carry value, not a greeting.
- **Scannable structure.** Short sections with clear subheads, generous spacing, one image max per section. People scan before they read.
- **One primary CTA button**, repeated at most twice (mid and end). Secondary links can live in a "more" footer, visually quieter.
- **Segment when you can.** Operators and Decision-Makers, or by sector — the same story told to the audience that recognises itself converts far better than a blast.
- **Cadence + consistency** beat volume. A recognisable structure each issue trains the open.
- **Plain-text-friendly.** Heavy image-only emails clip and trip spam filters; lead with text the point survives in.

## Messaging adaptation (how Glific shows up here)

- **The inbox is permission-based**, so warmth and usefulness lead. Open on the reader's program reality or a peer's story, not a product announcement.
- **Lead with the benefit.** Open on the value — time and effort saved, reach at scale — evidenced by a peer. We sell SaaS; the email earns its keep by being useful about the product and the program, not by selling belonging.
- **Community is a natural newsletter touchpoint — in proportion.** A newsletter is a good place to surface what the peer network is doing ("what NGOs built this month"), but community is one section, not the spine of every issue.
- **One story per issue.** Use the success narratives (proof-library §2) as the spine; don't cram three customers into one email.
- **Pricing emails** follow the pricing narrative (messaging §7): frame in program terms, anchor against the status quo, be transparent about the layers. Never a bare price drop.
- **Sequences** walk the arc across emails: problem → solution & benefit → plan (4 steps) → proof → CTA, with community as one beat. One arc beat per email.
- **Sage register.** Measured and explanatory, even in the subject and opener. Earn the open with a real number or situation, not a theatrical hook.

## Structure template

```
Subject: [≤50 chars, one idea, specific]
Preheader: [extends the subject, ≤90 chars]

[Opening — 1–2 sentences, the reader's reality or a peer moment. The point survives in the preview pane.]

## [Section 1 subhead — the story / the value]
[3–5 short lines. One idea. One sourced number or quote.]

[Primary CTA button — one verb, e.g. "See what your program looks like on Glific"]

## [Section 2 subhead — optional: community note / what's new / a resource]
[Short. Reinforces belonging or usefulness. Quieter.]

[Sign-off — human, from a named person where possible]
[Footer: secondary links, unsubscribe, address]
```

## Subject line patterns (with examples)

- **The number:** "From 2,500 to 420,000 — how Civis scaled a consultation" 
- **The peer:** "How a non-techie reached 3,000 girls on WhatsApp"
- **The relief:** "When one WhatsApp number isn't enough"
- **The community:** "What 120+ NGOs figured out this month"
- **The honest cost:** "What Glific actually costs (all of it)"

Avoid: "Newsletter #14," "Exciting updates from Glific," "You won't believe…", anything opening with "Glific."

> **Subject:** When one WhatsApp number isn't enough
> **Preheader:** How a program lead with no tech background reached 3,000+ girls across 54 schools.

## Newsletter validation (in addition to the three-layer pass)
- Subject ≤50 chars, one idea, no "Glific" opener, no clickbait?
- Preheader written (not auto-filled) and extends the subject?
- First sentence carries value in the preview pane?
- Leads with a benefit (community in proportion); one primary CTA, one idea, one proof?
- Sage register: no theatrical hook, no setup-then-reversal in subject or body?
- Scannable subheads, generous spacing, point survives without images?
